# Test Case - Usability

**Project:** Educational Metaverse – Battle of Itaparica (Decolonial Perspective)  
**Platform:** Multi-platform (Focus on VR Headset and Mobile Controls) via Spatial.io / Unity  
**Attribute:** Usability (ISO/IEC 25010)  
**Sub-attributes:** Appropriateness Recognizability, Learnability, and Operability  

---

## Objective

Verify the system's **Learnability** and **Operability** by ensuring a first-time student can intuitively master the navigation controls, easily identify the historical objective presented by the Maria Felipa NPC, and successfully complete a cultural minigame without external guidance or physical strain.

---

## Steps

1. **Onboarding & Tutorial:** Launch the metaverse room as a first-time user on a mobile device or VR headset. Observe the onboarding phase to see if a basic control overlay (joystick/teleportation mechanics) is presented.
2. **Objective Identification (Appropriateness Recognizability):** Walk the avatar toward the Maria Felipa NPC. Check if visual cues (such as an exclamation mark, a highlighted aura, or a distinct "Talk" button) clearly signal how to initiate the historical interaction.
3. **Dialogue & Navigation Execution:** Trigger the conversation with Maria Felipa to receive the first mission (e.g., locating a hidden historical asset on the island). Navigate the 3D environment toward the objective using the standard touch or controller layout.
4. **Minigame Engagement:** Interact with the designated historical asset to launch a cultural minigame. Attempt to complete the minigame using the on-screen or spatial instructions provided at the start of the challenge.
5. **UI Accessibility Check:** Open the main menu, inventory screen, and accessibility options during active gameplay to evaluate menu layout readability and ease of exit.

---

## Expected Result

* **Appropriateness Recognizability:** The student immediately recognizes interactive elements; Maria Felipa is instantly identifiable as a guide due to clear, contextual visual markers.
* **Learnability:** A first-time student spends less than 60 seconds adapting to the movement and interaction controls. The rules and UI prompts for the cultural minigame are clear, concise, and require no external explanation.
* **Operability:** The student navigates the island, interacts with NPCs, and completes the minigame smoothly with zero user confusion, minimal physical effort, and no accidental button inputs or interface trapping.
