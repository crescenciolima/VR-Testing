# Test Case – Usability

## Objective

Verify that a student who has never used the metaverse:

* Immediately understands the purpose of the main menus and buttons (**Appropriateness Recognizability**).
* Learns the basic controls and minigame mechanics independently (**Learnability**).
* Completes tasks without noticeable effort or confusion (**Operability**).

---

## Preconditions

* A student account has been created, but no progress has been saved.
* Updated Chrome browser running on a laptop with mouse and keyboard; internet connection ≥ 30 Mbps.
* No prior instructions are provided other than the invitation: **"Explore Itaparica Island."**
* Screen recording software is active for later analysis of clicks and task completion times.

---

## Steps

1. Access the metaverse URL.

2. Observe the home screen for up to 10 seconds without clicking anything.

   * Think aloud and describe what each icon or button appears to do.

3. Choose and click the button that appears to mean **"Enter"**.

4. Inside the 3D environment, use only the visual tooltips to:

   a. Walk to the NPC Maria Felipa.

   b. Interact with her and accept the first proposed minigame.

5. Complete the minigame until the score screen is displayed.

6. Record the total time elapsed between clicking **"Enter"** (Step 3) and reaching the end of the minigame.

7. Conduct a short questionnaire:

   * **"How easy was it to understand what to do?"** (Likert scale 1–5)
   * **"Would you have felt lost without these instructions?"** (Yes/No)

---

## Expected Result

* At least **80%** of participants correctly identify the primary buttons during Step 2.
* **100%** of participants are able to reach Maria Felipa and start the minigame within **2 minutes**, without external assistance.
* During the minigame:

  * No repeated incorrect clicks (> 2).
  * No unintentional returns to the main menu.
* Total completion time through Step 5 is **≤ 5 minutes**.
* Average ease-of-use rating is **≥ 4/5**.
* No more than **10%** of participants answer **"Yes"** to feeling lost without instructions.

---

## Success Criteria

The test is **approved** if all expected results are achieved and the majority of first-time users can successfully navigate, interact, and complete the initial learning experience without assistance.

The test is **rejected** if:

* More than 20% of participants fail to identify the primary controls.
* Any participant requires external help to start the first minigame.
* The average usability rating falls below 4/5.
* More than 10% of participants report feeling lost during the experience.
